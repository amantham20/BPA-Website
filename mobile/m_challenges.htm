EPA

What is EPA?

The EPA(Environmental Protection Agency) is a public entity that seeks to protect the health of human beings and the environment. The department is responsible for environmental protection. It achieves its objectives by developing and enforcing rules and regulations for enterprises, states and other entities

What Is a Solar Power Purchase Agreement (SPPA)?

The Solar Power Purchase Agreement (SPPA) is a financing arrangement whereby the third party photovoltaic (PV) system is owned, operated and maintained and a host customer agrees to place the system on the property and buy the electric output of the system on from the solar service provider for a predetermined period of time.

 

Zoning

A zoning ordinance is a written regulation and law defining how property can be used in certain geographical areas. Zoning dictates whether areas may be used for residential or business purposes, and may also regulate batch size, positioning, volume (or density) and structural height.

Technology & Appliances

Renewables are not included in local zoning legislation in several municipalities and are therefore considered to have prohibited in use. In order to implement such technology a variance of use is necessary. This subjects such technology to a rigorous assessment of aesthetics, safety and location in addition to being costly and time-consuming.

 

Permits & Insurance

To ensure that homeowners meet the US Green Building Council (USGBC) or LEED ® certifications, it is a crucial step in the initial planning to check your insurance policy. The cost of replacing green upgrades to your home could not be covered by a regular homeowner insurance policy. Confirm your householder policy specifically provides substitution to prevent you from paying out - of-pocket costs in order to meet the same standards. A policy on green homes includes either the actual cash value or replacement costs to restore a green home approved or to upgrade a regular house to a green standard.
